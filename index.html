Welcome to Wenlin's Homepage!

I am a Ph.D. student in Transportation Engineering at Arizona State University, working on large-scale optimization, operations research, and machine learning for transportation systems. I previously received my B.S. in Computer Science from the University of Utah.

My research spans large-scale network modeling, transportation planning, decomposition algorithms, and ML for scientific computing. I am currently collaborating with Argonne National Laboratory on national-scale freight rail network optimization.

I am actively seeking a Summer 2027 research / applied scientist internship in ML, optimization, or fleet & marketplace decision systems. Feel free to reach out.

Research Interests

My work sits at the intersection of optimization and machine learning. Recent directions include:

  • Large-scale optimization & OR — column generation, Lagrangian relaxation, and Branch-and-Price for network design and routing.
  • Network modeling — time-space networks and multi-commodity flow with decomposition for freight and mobility systems.
  • Transportation planning — freight rail routing, blocking, and fleet decision support at national scale.
  • ML for scientific computing — PINNs, neural ODEs, and learned differential operators for PDE solvers.
